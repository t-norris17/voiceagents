## Can I take a loan against my 401(k)?
**Yes — the Vertex plan allows participant loans.** You borrow from your own vested balance and
pay yourself back, with interest, through payroll deductions. It is not a taxable withdrawal as
long as you repay it on schedule.

## How much you can borrow
- **Minimum:** $1,000.
- **Maximum:** the **lesser of $50,000 or 50% of your vested account balance** (the $50,000 cap is
  reduced by your highest loan balance in the past 12 months).
- **One loan outstanding at a time.** You must pay off an existing loan before taking a new one.

## Terms and cost
- **Repayment term:** up to **5 years** (up to **15 years** if the loan is to buy your primary
  residence).
- **Interest rate:** **Prime + 1%**, fixed for the life of the loan. The interest you pay goes
  back into **your own account**.
- **Fees:** a **$75 origination fee** and a **$25/year maintenance fee**, deducted from your account.
- **Repayment:** automatic **after-tax payroll deductions**, usually starting the pay period after
  the loan is issued.

## Important things to know
- **Loans reduce your invested balance** while outstanding, so that money isn't in the market.
- **If you leave Vertex** with a loan outstanding, the remaining balance generally becomes due. If
  it isn't repaid, it's treated as a **loan offset** — taxable, and subject to a **10% early-
  withdrawal penalty if you're under 59½**. You can avoid the tax by rolling an amount equal to the
  offset into an IRA or new plan by your tax-filing deadline (including extensions).
- Missing payments can cause the loan to **default** and become a taxable distribution.

## How to request a loan
Model and request a loan on the portal at **nesteggu.com** (Loans → Request a Loan), or call the
**NestEgg participant line at 1-800-555-0148**. You'll see the amount available to you, the
payment schedule, and the rate before you confirm.

## Common questions
- *How much can I borrow?* Lesser of $50,000 or 50% of your vested balance.
- *Can I have two loans?* No — one at a time on this plan.
- *What if I leave with a loan?* It becomes due; unpaid amounts are offset and may be taxed/penalized.
- *Is loan interest tax-deductible?* No — 401(k) loan interest is not deductible.
