# Vertex Manufacturing 401(k) — Rolling Money Into the Plan

**Recordkeeper:** NestEgg Retirement Services · **Plan:** Vertex Manufacturing 401(k) (Plan 001)

> Knowledge-base article for the voice agent. General plan rules. **Synthetic demo content.**

## Can I roll over funds from a previous retirement plan into this one?
**Yes — the Vertex plan accepts roll-ins.** Consolidating an old account into your Vertex 401(k)
keeps everything in one place, under one set of fees and one login.

## What the plan accepts
- **Prior employer plans:** 401(k), 403(b), and governmental 457(b).
- **Traditional IRAs** (pre-tax).
- **Both pre-tax and Roth** balances are accepted (Roth amounts keep their Roth status).
- The money must be an **eligible rollover distribution** — most vested retirement balances qualify.
  Required minimum distributions and certain hardship amounts are **not** eligible.

## Direct vs. indirect rollover (this matters)
- **Direct rollover (recommended):** the old provider sends the money straight to NestEgg. **No
  taxes withheld**, nothing owed.
- **Indirect rollover:** the old provider pays *you*, and you have **60 days** to deposit it into
  the Vertex plan. The provider withholds **20%** for taxes, and you have to make that 20% up from
  your own pocket to roll the full amount — so direct is almost always better.

## How to start a roll-in
1. Call the **NestEgg Rollover Concierge at 1-800-555-0148** (or start it on the portal under
   **Rollovers → Roll Money In**). They'll give you the paperwork and the exact mailing/wiring
   instructions for your old provider.
2. Contact your **previous provider** and request a **direct rollover** to the Vertex Manufacturing
   401(k), Plan 001, at NestEgg.
3. Once the funds arrive, they're invested per your current elections (or the default target-date
   fund). You'll see the deposit on the portal.

## Good to know
- There's **no waiting period** — you can roll money in at any time, even before you're eligible
  for the match.
- Rolled-in money is **always 100% yours** (roll-in balances are fully vested).
- Rolling in does **not** count against your annual contribution limit.

## Common questions
- *Which accounts can I roll in?* Old 401(k)/403(b)/457(b) and traditional IRAs; pre-tax and Roth.
- *Will I owe taxes?* Not on a direct rollover.
- *Can I roll in a Roth IRA?* Roth **IRA** money generally cannot be rolled into a 401(k); Roth
  **401(k)** money can. Check with the Rollover Concierge.
- *How long does it take?* Usually 1–3 weeks, depending on your old provider.
