## I'm leaving Vertex — how does this affect my plan?
Your **vested** account balance is always yours to keep. When you leave, contributions stop, but
your money stays invested until you decide what to do with it. You have four main options.

## Your four options
1. **Leave it in the Vertex plan.** Allowed if your **vested balance is $7,000 or more**. It keeps
   growing tax-deferred, but you can no longer contribute or take new loans.
2. **Roll it into your new employer's plan** (if they accept roll-ins) — a **direct rollover**,
   no taxes.
3. **Roll it into an IRA** — a direct rollover to an IRA of your choice, no taxes; often the widest
   investment selection.
4. **Take a cash distribution** — the money is paid to you, but it's **taxed as ordinary income**,
   plus a **10% early-withdrawal penalty if you're under 59½** (see exceptions below). Usually the
   most expensive option.

## Small balances are handled automatically
- **Under $1,000:** the plan may **automatically cash you out**.
- **$1,000 to $7,000:** the plan may **automatically roll your balance into a NestEgg Safe Harbor
  IRA** in your name if you don't choose an option.
- **$7,000 or more:** you can leave it in the plan.

## Vesting when you leave
- Your own contributions: **100% yours**.
- Employer match: vested on a **3-year cliff** — fully yours at 3 years of service, otherwise the
  unvested portion is **forfeited** when you leave.

## If you have an outstanding loan
Leaving with a loan means the balance generally **becomes due**. If it isn't repaid, it's treated
as a **loan offset** — taxable, and subject to the 10% penalty if you're under 59½. You can avoid
the tax by rolling an amount equal to the offset into an IRA or new plan by your **tax-filing
deadline** (including extensions).

## Age-based exceptions to the 10% penalty
- **Age 55+ separation ("Rule of 55"):** if you leave Vertex in or after the year you turn **55**,
  distributions from **this plan** are not subject to the 10% penalty (they're still taxed as
  income). This applies to the workplace plan, not IRAs.
- **Age 59½:** no early-withdrawal penalty on any option.
- **Required Minimum Distributions** must begin at **age 73**.

## How to start
Call the **NestEgg participant line at 1-800-555-0148** or use the portal (**My Account →
Distributions**) to model each option and start a rollover or distribution. Consider speaking with
a tax advisor before taking cash.

## Common questions
- *Can I leave my money in the plan?* Yes, if your vested balance is $7,000+.
- *Will I be taxed if I roll it over?* Not on a direct rollover to an IRA or new plan.
- *I'm 56 and leaving — is there a penalty if I take some cash?* Under the Rule of 55, distributions
  from this plan avoid the 10% penalty (still taxed as income).
- *What happens to my loan?* It becomes due; unpaid amounts are offset and may be taxed/penalized.
